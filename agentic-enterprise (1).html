THE OFFICIAL FUTURE — AND WHAT IT MISSES

The future is often assumed to be a continuation of the past

Most planning quietly defaults to one "official future" — a midpoint extrapolation, conservative enough to feel safe, narrow enough to miss what actually matters.

The Zone of Undiscussability
Above and below the official future lies the zone of undiscussability — the futures we won't seriously entertain because they feel impolite, alarming, or career-limiting. This is where the disruption almost always comes from.
Considering Alternatives
Generate options and test strategies. Sense opportunities and risks. Accelerate decisions. Foresight isn't prediction — it's organized peripheral vision.

CAPABILITIES · THE PATTERN

When marginal information costs change, new disruption follows

Each computing era is defined by what gets radically cheaper to do with information — and what becomes newly possible as a result. The pattern is consistent: an information-cost collapse precedes a wave of disruption that wasn't visible from inside the old regime.

The Agentic Era
If GenAI made manipulation of information cheap, agentic AI makes coordination cheap — across information, systems, people, and markets. That's a different order of disruption.

"Kmart and Walmart got access to the bar code in the same year." Same input. Radically different responses. Same opening exists now.

CAPABILITIES · THE DRIVERS

AI isn't just growing — it's propelled by four independent exponentials

Each driver compounds independently. The combined trajectory is steeper than any one of them. Betting against the rate of improvement means betting against four exponentials at once.

1
Compute Density
Hardware performance per dollar — chip die improvements, specialized silicon, memory bandwidth.
2
Cluster Scale
Infrastructure volume — datacenter buildouts, networking fabric, power delivery, interconnect topology.
3
Data Frontiers
Input expansion — synthetic data, multimodal corpora, RL environments, real-world deployment feedback.
4
Algorithmic ROI
Software efficiency — better training methods, smarter architectures, more capability per FLOP.
CAPABILITIES · WHAT'S UNEVEN

The jagged frontier: superhuman in some domains, brittle in others

Reinforcement learning has driven dramatic gains in domains where output is specifiable and verifiable. Where neither holds — leadership, strategy, ethical judgment — progress is slower and harder to measure.

The Three Conditions
Legible. Specifiable. Verifiable. Domains that satisfy all three are racing ahead. Domains that satisfy none stay stubbornly human.
"OpenAI's free Advanced Voice Mode will fumble the dumbest questions in your Instagram reels — and at the same time, OpenAI's highest-tier Codex model will go off for an hour to coherently restructure an entire code base."
Industry observation, 2026
CAPABILITIES · THE HARNESS

AI-in-use will improve as the agentic harness matures

The LLM is just the engine. What makes it useful is everything bolted around it — tool use, orchestration, context engineering, safety. 100 years ago, a "car" was a park bench, three bicycle tires, and a bunch of hot, spinning metal. The harness was missing. We're in the same moment now.

The Engine
LLM capability — improving on the four exponentials. But raw capability ≠ deployable capability.
The Harness Gap
Most enterprise AI failures aren't model failures. They're harness failures — bad context, brittle tools, missing safety, no orchestration. This is where the next wave of value lives.
CAPABILITIES · THE UNIT OF WORK

An agentic work unit closes the loop: reason · act · verify

An agent isn't just a chatbot with tools. It's a closed loop of cognition and action. When all three components work — and only when all three work — the agent can be trusted to complete a task without a human re-doing it.

Reason
Can the agent plan a path from where things are to where the goal is? Can it decompose, prioritize, and revise?
Act
Can it execute in the actual systems where work lives? Or is it a brilliant strategist trapped in a sandbox?
Verify
Can it tell if it succeeded? Without verification, you're just outsourcing your work to a confident hallucinator.
WORK · THE QUESTION

In an agentic enterprise, how might work be restructured?

The org chart has been remarkably stable for a century. It's about to bend in ways the spreadsheet won't show, because the unit of production is no longer just the employee. The question isn't "how many people do we need?" — it's "what work belongs where?"

Configuration 1
Human Employees
The status quo. Work flows through people; agents are bolted-on assistants if they exist at all.
Configuration 2
Humans + AI Assistants
Each person amplified by a personal AI layer. Boundaries of jobs shift but reporting lines stay roughly intact.
Configuration 3
Humans + Agents
Agents become first-class operators in the org. People do the work only agents can't — judgment, taste, relationship, responsibility.
Underneath the ratio question
What might your org look like if you could target the highest costs in your business, remove system bottlenecks, and add new activities that were never viable before? The right question isn't "what's the human-to-agent ratio?" — it's "what work suddenly becomes possible?"
WORK · THE EMERGING STRUCTURE

A layer of humans + agents wrapped around an autonomic core

A useful analogy: the body. The autonomic nervous system handles heartbeat, breathing, digestion — the optimization and coordination that doesn't need conscious thought. The cerebral cortex handles meaning, judgment, and taste. Organizations are starting to look the same.

Autonomic Core
Optimizing, coordinating, executing. Verifiable tasks. Continuous operation. No need for a human to be in every loop — and a strong case that humans should not be.
Meaning-Making Layer
Judgment, taste, ambiguity resolution. Humans setting direction, supplying values, taking responsibility — augmented by agents but not replaced by them.

WORK · WHY HUMANS REMAIN ESSENTIAL

As agents gain capability, human work doesn't disappear — it concentrates

Reason 1
Responsibility is human
Once you're making judgments, judgments come with responsibilities. And responsibility lies with humans at the end of the day.
— Joy Buolamwini
Reason 2
Humans rarely know what they actually want
Stating top-level goals isn't sufficient. At each stage, new decision points are entailed and new preferences revealed. Taste cannot be fractally unrolled from a few bits of initial input.
Reason 3
Humans provide useful friction
"Managers didn't just move information around — they edited it, and decided what mattered. A world model replaces that editorial function with something that feels like judgment but isn't."
— Nate B Jones
Reason 4
Humanness may become scarce — and therefore valuable
A post-commodity economy may be emerging, where a growing share of expenditure goes toward goods and services whose value is inseparable from the human who provided them.
"In the intelligence era, we'll have to separate the parts of work that are about meaning from the parts that are just busywork — and let machines handle the busywork."
— Sam Altman
WORK · THE NEW FACTOR OF PRODUCTION

Token consumption is exploding — but we lack measurement and policy tools

If labor and capital were the factors of production for the industrial enterprise, then tokens — units of AI compute consumed — are becoming a third factor for the agentic one. The economics aren't yet legible inside most P&Ls.

What's missing
Token budgets at the individual contributor level. Most orgs can tell you headcount cost per team. Very few can tell you the marginal cost of an agent-completed task — or how that's changing month over month.
The governance question
If every engineer is amplified 10× through tokens, then the difference between a high-output and low-output engineer compounds. How do you allocate the budget? Who is accountable for the bill? These are real questions without standard answers yet.
"Every single engineer in our company will need an annual token budget. They're gonna make a few hundred thousand dollars a year their base pay. I'm gonna give them probably half of that on top of it as tokens so that they could be amplified 10×."
— Jensen Huang, GTC 2026
CUSTOMERS · ADOPTION IS ALREADY HERE

Personal AI use is emerging rapidly — and most of it isn't work

The 2026 customer is not the 2024 customer. They've spent eighteen months talking to AI several times a day, often about things they would not have brought to a search engine, a doctor, or a friend.

1/3
of Americans interact with AI several times a day or almost constantly
>70%
of ChatGPT messages are now non-work-related
What this means for incumbents
Customers are forming behavioral patterns and brand intimacies with AI systems before any of your products have an AI strategy. The reference experience for "intelligent product" is being set somewhere else.

Imagine if, in 2006, you'd known how the combination of search, mobile apps, and social media would revolutionize how businesses connected to customers. You're standing in the equivalent moment now.

The hacker signal
Early adopters are wiring up personal AI stacks themselves — Claude into Discord servers, custom agents running their email triage, voice models routing their calendar. The infrastructure is forming bottom-up, faster than any enterprise vendor can ship.
"I wired Claude into my Discord bot in a weekend. Runs my server now."
Reddit / r/AI_Agents
Pew Research Center (McClain et al., Apr 2025) · NBER Working Paper No. 34255 (Chatterji et al., Sep 2025)
CUSTOMERS · THE REFRAME

An "unmet needs" lens reveals "good enough" AI in a new light

If you measure AI against expert humans, it often falls short. If you measure it against access — the experience of those for whom the expert human was never available — the picture inverts. AI doesn't have to be best. It has to be available.

Unmet Need 01
Healthcare
A primary-care physician you can talk to at 2 AM about a worrying symptom, in your language, without a co-pay or three-week wait. Not better than the best doctor — but better than no doctor.
Unmet Need 02
Therapy & Companionship
A consistent presence that remembers what you said yesterday, costs nothing per session, and is reachable from inside your worst moment. The clinical objections are valid. The behavioral pattern is forming anyway.
Unmet Need 03
Religion & Meaning
Spiritual guidance, ritual continuity, scripture interpretation, end-of-life conversation. Areas where institutional decline left a hole, and AI is increasingly being asked to fill it — for better and worse.
The strategic implication
"Good enough AI for the underserved" is not a smaller market than "best-in-class AI for the well-served." It may be the larger one, and it is forming below most enterprise radars. The product category that will define the next decade may not be one any incumbent is currently building.
CUSTOMERS · POWER REDISTRIBUTION

Agents shift power to consumers and smaller players

Most consumers don't optimize every dollar, every day. Agentic AI breaks that equation — the cost of comparison shopping, complaint resolution, and counter-offer drafting collapses toward zero. The asymmetries that defined consumer markets quietly invert.

Shift 01
Less loyalty
When an agent compares your rates against everyone else's nightly — and switches you without friction — sticky-by-default business models stop being sticky.
McKinsey, "The end of inertia: agentic AI's disruption of retail and SME banking"
Shift 02
Better service
A controlled study found 49% of AI-assisted customer complaints received relief, compared to 40% for human-filed ones — the agent is more patient, more persistent, and never loses the script.
Minkuo Shin, City University of Hong Kong
Shift 03
Better negotiation
Users who used AI to negotiate car purchases and apartment rentals saw significant performance improvements. The negotiation premium that informed buyers used to capture is being democratized.
Ryan Shea, Columbia University
The second-order question
If a meaningful share of customer decisions starts running through agents — comparing, negotiating, switching — then your customer is no longer your direct customer. Your marketing reaches the agent first. Your loyalty programs reach the agent first. Your customer service is judged by the agent first. What does brand even mean in that environment?
CUSTOMERS · THE NEW MARKET TOPOLOGY

Agents require a scaled response from agentic enterprises

Three new market quadrants are opening up — categories that didn't exist as named entities a year ago. Each represents a new interface, a new protocol, and a new addressable market.

Provider entity, rows

  • B2A. Selling to agents acting on behalf of users. Your product, ranked and chosen by an algorithm representing a customer.
  • C2A. Consumers operating within personal-AI environments — wearables, ambient interfaces, persistent assistants.
  • A2B / A2C. Your own agents reaching customer agents, customer humans, partner agents.
  • A2A. Agent-to-agent commerce, negotiation, and coordination. Protocol-mediated economic activity.
The category gap
Most enterprises have a strategy for one quadrant — usually B2C or B2B. Almost none have an explicit strategy for being chosen by an agent, communicating with an agent, or selling another agent's output downstream. That's where the next decade of growth — and the next decade of margin compression — will play out.
LEADING · WHAT TO DO NOW

Leaders work in two registers simultaneously: adopting and adapting

Adopting is operational — capturing today's ROI inside existing workflows. Adapting is structural — preparing the organization for a world where agents are first-class participants. Most leadership teams are good at one. The job is doing both at once.

ADOPTING — Capture the obvious ROI
1. Develop AI literacy — and require it
Build your own AI fluency. Require it from your team. Without leader-level intuition, you'll get talked into the wrong bets and out of the right ones.
2. Invest in obvious ROI opportunities
Inside existing workflows. The 80% cases that compound to real margin. Don't moonshot before the ground game works.
3. Make data actionable
Smart unification, semantic modeling, governance. Agents are only as useful as the data substrate they reason over.
4. Develop experimental muscle
Adaptive mindset. Faster cycles of trial and discard than your finance partner is comfortable with.
5. Track emerging capabilities
Expect to be surprised. The jagged frontier shifts faster than quarterly planning cycles assume.
ADAPTING — Prepare for what's actually coming
1. Communicate honestly about jobs
Recognize AI disruption of tasks, jobs, and systems. Communicate clearly: all jobs are not going away — but many will change shape. The wrong message is denial. The other wrong message is panic.
2. Evolve toward the autonomic core
And the humans + agents layer. Restructure work, not just tools. The org chart is the strategy.
3. Make customer-centricity the purpose
Of your agentic enterprise. Use the new tools to solve the coordination problem that journey maps never could.
4. Experiment with new differentiation
Future sources of competitive advantage — being chosen by agents, partnering with personal AIs, owning A2A protocols.
5. Prepare your scaled response to personal AI
Powerful and pervasive. Assume customers will increasingly arrive at your business through an intermediary they trust more than they trust you.
THE TAKEAWAY

The Agentic Enterprise isn't a product strategy. It's an organizational strategy.

"The clerical task, writing down transactions and reconciling ledgers, is a weak bundle. The accountant's job is a strong bundle. She interprets tax law as it applies to a specific client, signs the audit that the bank and the SEC will rely on, and carries the legal exposure."
— Luis Garicano, The Task is not the Job
The Shift
From "AI as feature" to AI as organizational substrate
Agents are not a new SKU. They're a new mode of getting work done — and the org that uses them well will look different from the one that doesn't.
The Principle
Separate strong bundles from weak ones
Weak bundles (clerical, repeatable, verifiable) move to agents. Strong bundles (judgment, responsibility, taste, relationship) concentrate value in humans.
The Discipline
Both registers, simultaneously
Capture the obvious ROI today. Restructure for the world you don't yet see. The temptation is to do only one. The winners will do both.

The official future says AI will be useful. The futures that actually arrive will be stranger, faster, and structurally different from that. The work is to take them seriously before they're consensus.
